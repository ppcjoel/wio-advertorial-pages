The "Double Label" Explained

Consumers receiving the product frequently report confusion over the packaging. The chip bag arrives with two distinct nutritional labels. The first label looks like a standard FDA nutritional table displaying typical chip calories and carbs. Right next to it sits a second sticker labeled "Digestibility-Corrected" showcasing the fraction of calories your body actually processes.

This dual-labeling system is a direct result of strict FDA compliance. Under the Nutrition Labeling and Education Act (NLEA) of 1993, regulatory bodies mandate that companies list the total amount of ingredients physically present in the food bag. The FDA, however, allows for "corrected" secondary labeling when clinical data proves that those raw ingredients are not actually bioavailable to the consumer.

The government essentially permits WiO to correct the outdated math of the standard table. Because the botanical extract blocks the digestive enzymes, the vast majority of the calories listed on the standard label never enter the bloodstream. The "Double Label" operates as a transparent testimony to the clinical evidence proving these chips behave completely differently inside the human gut.
