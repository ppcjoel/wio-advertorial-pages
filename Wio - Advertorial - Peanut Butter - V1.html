The "Double Label" Explained

Consumers receiving the product frequently report confusion over the packaging. The peanut butter jar arrives with two distinct nutritional labels. The first label looks like a standard FDA nutritional table displaying high fat and high calories. Right next to it sits a second sticker labeled "Digestibility-Corrected" showcasing the 31-calorie count.

This dual-labeling system is a direct result of strict FDA compliance. Under the Nutrition Labeling and Education Act (NLEA) of 1993, regulatory bodies mandate that companies list the total amount of ingredients physically present in the food jar. The FDA, however, allows for "corrected" secondary labeling when clinical data proves that those raw ingredients are not actually bioavailable to the consumer.

The government essentially permits WiO to correct the outdated math of the standard table. Because the botanical extract blocks the fat enzymes, the vast majority of the calories listed on the standard label never enter the bloodstream. The "Double Label" operates as a transparent testimony to the clinical evidence proving the peanut butter behaves differently inside the human gut.
