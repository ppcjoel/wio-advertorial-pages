How Switching to One Simple Bread Helped Me Take Back My Health at 54

After years of feeling stuck and scared about my blood sugar numbers, I found something in a Utah bakery that changed my mornings completely.

Karen Ellsworth, Health & Wellness Writer
April 2, 2026 · 7 min read

I still remember sitting in my doctor's office two years ago, staring at a printout of my lab results while she explained what "pre-diabetic range" meant. My fasting blood sugar had been creeping up for three years straight, and I'd just crossed the line. I was 52, I was tired all the time, and I felt like my body was giving up on me.

The first thing she told me? "We need to talk about your carbs." And honestly, my heart sank. Because if you know me, you know I'm a bread person. Toast with butter in the morning. A sandwich at lunch. A roll with dinner. Bread was the background music of every meal I ate.

The Bread Problem Nobody Talks About

Here's what I learned in the weeks after that appointment: a single slice of regular white bread can contain 13 to 15 grams of net carbs. Two slices of whole wheat for a sandwich? That's nearly 30 grams right there. For someone watching their carb intake, that's a huge portion of the daily budget gone before you've even thought about what goes between the slices.

My doctor explained that refined carbohydrates can cause rapid spikes in blood sugar, which over time may contribute to metabolic stress. She suggested I look into a low-carb or ketogenic approach to support healthy blood sugar levels already within normal range. The research she pointed me to was compelling — multiple studies have shown that reducing carbohydrate intake can support healthy metabolic function.

Did You Know?

According to the CDC, over 96 million American adults have blood sugar levels in the pre-diabetic range, and more than 80% of them don't know it. Dietary changes, particularly reducing refined carbohydrates, are among the most commonly recommended lifestyle modifications.

So I did what any determined person would do. I quit bread cold turkey. And for about three weeks, I was miserable.

I Tried Every Substitute. Most Were Awful.

I tried lettuce wraps (soggy, sad). I tried cloud bread (fine for a day, boring by day two). I bought three different brands of "keto bread" from the grocery store, and each one tasted like cardboard held together with hope and xanthan gum. One brand was so dense I could have used it as a doorstop.

The worst part wasn't even the taste. It was the feeling of deprivation. Everyone else at the table was eating normal bread, and I was picking at some crumbly substitute that fell apart the second you tried to spread anything on it. I started to dread meals. And when you dread meals, it's only a matter of time before you give up entirely.

Then My Sister Sent Me a Link

It was a random Tuesday night. My sister Linda, who lives in Salt Lake City, texted me: "Have you tried this bread? It's made here in Utah and it's actually good." She included a link to something called WiO SmartFoods and their keto bread.

I almost ignored it. I'd been burned too many times. But Linda is not the type to recommend things lightly — she's pickier about food than anyone I know. So I ordered a loaf.

When it arrived, I was skeptical. I popped two slices in the toaster, spread on some butter and a little sugar-free jam, and took a bite. I actually stopped chewing for a second because I couldn't believe it. It tasted like bread. Real bread. The kind of bread I'd been mourning for months.
